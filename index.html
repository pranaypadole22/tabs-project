history

Ancient Period: Raigad Fort, located in the Sahyadri mountain range in Maharashtra, has a long history. It was originally known as Rairi and was under the control of various local chiefs and kingdoms before the rise of the Marathas.

Maratha Empire: The fort is most famous for being the capital of the Maratha Empire under Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. In 1674, Shivaji was coronated as the Chhatrapati (King) of the Maratha Empire at Raigad Fort, marking the fort's prominence in Indian history. It served as the base for the Maratha administration and military operations.

Strategic Importance: The fort's location on a hilltop made it a formidable defense post. It has several significant structures, including the King’s Palace, the Queen’s quarters, the Balekilla (upper fort), and the Samadhi (memorial) of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj.

British Period: After the fall of the Maratha Empire, the British took over the fort in 1818, marking the end of its use as a military stronghold.

vision

Raigad Fort is envisioned as a symbol of Maratha pride and heritage. Efforts are focused on preserving and restoring the fort to maintain its historical significance and to educate future generations about its role in Indian history.

The fort is also seen as a key site for tourism, with plans to enhance its accessibility and facilities while maintaining its cultural integrity.

goals

Conservation: Restore and preserve the fort’s structures, ensuring that the architectural and historical elements are maintained for future generations.

Tourism Development: Develop Raigad Fort as a major tourist destination by improving infrastructure, providing educational resources, and creating visitor-friendly amenities, such as guided tours and informative displays.

Cultural Promotion: Promote the history and legacy of the Maratha Empire through cultural events, exhibitions, and Sustainability: educational programs at the fort. Ensure that all development and restoration activities are sustainable, minimizing environmental impact and preserving the natural beauty of the surrounding area.

Historical Research: Encourage historical research and archaeological studies to uncover more about the fort’s past, contributing to a deeper understanding of its significance.
